  Feature 1  ┬╖  Priority: HIGH
  Intelligent UBO Graph Extraction & Verification
  "Stop manually tracing beneficial ownership. Let AI map the corporate tree automatically."
  (a) User Pain Point
  For KYB on corporates, officers spend hours parsing multi-page registry documents in different languages to identify UBOs, directors, and ownership chains especially helpful for multi-jurisdiction entities. This is the #1 bottleneck in corporate onboarding.
  (b) How It Works
  LLM + OCR pipeline ingests uploaded registry documents (in any language). AI extracts entity names, roles, ownership %, and jurisdiction. Outputs a structured UBO graph that auto-populates Sancus's KYB fields. Confidence scores flag ambiguous data for officer review. ΓÇ£All extracted entities, relationships, and ownership percentages are versioned with source citations, supporting FATF and EU audit expectations for traceability.ΓÇ¥
  (c) Impact on Client Experience
  Corporate onboarding time cut by 50ΓÇô60% for complex entities. Officers review a structured graph rather than raw PDFs. Downstream: graph data feeds AML checks automatically, reducing manual AML trigger setup. Clients experience dramatically fewer "we need more documents" requests.
  (d) Prioritization Rationale
  Highest ROI opportunity in Sancus today. UBO complexity is the #1 pain point in corporate KYB globally. Sancus already has AI document extraction for some fields ΓÇö this extends the existing capability. Builds on norbloc's existing registry data source integrations.

  Feature 2  ┬╖  Priority: HIGH
  Adaptive AI Risk Scoring Engine
  "Risk scores that learn from your decisions ΓÇö not just a static formula you set once."
  (a) User Pain Point
  Sancus's current risk scoring is based on static rules and parameters set during implementation. Over time, as patterns emerge in officer approval/rejection decisions, the model becomes stale. Officers override scores frequently without feedback looping back into the model.
  (b) How It Works
  ML model trained on historical case outcomes (approved, rejected, flagged for EDD). Ingests all structured risk factors (country risk, PEP status, document quality, source of funds). The model retrains within a governed MLOps pipeline, with drift monitoring, bias checks, and humanΓÇæinΓÇætheΓÇæloop approval thatΓÇÖs aligned with EU AI Act expectations for highΓÇærisk AI systems.
  (c) Impact on Client Experience
  Risk scoring becomes a living intelligence layer, not a static policy. Officers spend less time on false positives. High-risk cases are surfaced with clear reasoning, reducing decision time. Clients in low-risk segments see faster approvals. Auditability improves since every score has an AI-generated explanation.
  (d) Prioritization Rationale
  Strong priority: risk scoring is core to Sancus's compliance value prop. Explainable AI is increasingly required by regulators (EU AI Act). Norbloc already stores rich case outcome data so the training data exists. Medium engineering lift but very high client value and defensibility. This module will require formal technical documentation and governance under the EU AI Act as a highΓÇærisk AI system.

  Feature 3  ┬╖  Priority: MEDIUM
  Conversational AML Alert Triage Copilot
  "Instead of reading 40 PEP hits, ask: 'Which of these actually need my attention today?'"
  (a) User Pain Point
  AML screening generates high volumes of alerts, most of which are false positives. Officers spend significant time manually reviewing each hit to determine relevance. This creates fatigue, increases error rate, and slows down onboarding especially for common names in high-PEP regions like MENA.
  (b) How It Works
  LLM-powered triage layer sits on top of existing PEP/sanctions screening results. Officer can query: "Show me only hits with strong name match AND active sanctions" or "Summarize all adverse media for this entity." AI provides advisoryΓÇæmode triage suggestions (likely false positive / needs review / escalate) with justification and citations, while officers retain full decision authority.
  (c) Impact on Client Experience
  Officers review only high-confidence alerts (est. 60ΓÇô70% reduction in manual review volume). Triage decisions are faster and more consistent. New officers onboard faster with AI guidance. Audit trail includes AI triage rationale alongside officer decision, strengthening compliance documentation.
  (d) Prioritization Rationale
  Medium priority: high user value, but requires careful validation to avoid false negatives in a regulatory context. Builds naturally on Sancus's existing AML integration layer. Should follow Feature 1 and 2 in roadmap. Potential differentiator: few KYC platforms offer AI-assisted AML triage today. Initial rollout should run in shadow/advisory mode only, to validate no increase in false negatives before influencing production workflows.
